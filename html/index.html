A little bit about me

Working on my way

Born in Dominican Republic, Santo Domingo. When I was a children I was always told that I can be a civil engineer or arquitech because I was good at drawing. When I enrolled in university I understand that I didn't have a good math base, so studying civil engineer was going to take me longer than it should be, I changed to industrial design, a career that didn't even know existed. I didn't have much of a guidence growing up, I was raise to be a good person but without a concrete purpose or how important it was to be stable in the future. Now days I have to find my self and work to not fall behind.

As an student back in 2011:

      My favorite part during university was 3d modeling, althought this aspect wasn't explore deep enough.
      I'm was good at drawing since I was child, so most of the courses relating drawing and digital drawing were easy for me.

Project Year Brief explanation
2013

Both projects were complete during my years as student, the first one in 2013, was a concept for a toiler for people with back issues or elders, one of my team members thought it would take us too much time to complete a better option to present, so we went with a version in my opinion, bad, but anyways I was able to practice more about curving in SolidWorks, which is the software that I use most of the time.

The second project was a blindcane (smart cane), that have a sensor to dectect areas that the usual cane can't, allowing blind people to feel more safe while walking.

In my first presentation for this project I got nervous and couldn't continue, it was infront of a lot of people, it was really embarrasing, so I decided with my group for the last presentation to not be presented, as it wasn't really required. I was happy with the result of my model, but the person we hired to do the wiring, and the sensor function did a bad job, making our project difficult to present, some of my members cried but I was just relieve it ended. I never thought the project have any future.

A project that I really enjoyed was at INICA , there I did an air conditioning grille, basically the first one in the country, simple but, it felt like a real thing, with a real purpose compare to all the things I did in university, and that I saw other classmates doing. Some of my work at INICA can been seen at the gallery.
